WASHINGTON SQUARE (UN AIR DE BANJO)

“Washington Square" by the Village Stompers out of New York City was a big instrumental hit in 1963. It had a significant part for banjo and did not originally include lyrics.

A French version of the song, « Un air de banjo », has appropriately trans-Atlantic lyrics and was popularized by singer Sacha Distel. In it, a musician who composes on banjo falls asleep over his instrument to the sound of winter rain like music falling on the roof.

In his dreams, the musician's music becomes an increasingly huge success in America, all to win the love of a woman he knows back in the real world. Painfully, however, the woman happens upon him back in reality and leaves laughing.

The song ends with the musician crying but manages to snatch victory from the clutches of defeat: Unbeknownst to him, the door is opening: his love has had second thoughts and is returning.

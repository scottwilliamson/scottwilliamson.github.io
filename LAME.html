LA MER (BEYOND THE SEA)

Charles Trenet's 1946 « La mer » (written years earlier) is a rapturous observation of the beauty of a seaside vista, complete with ponds, damp reeds, rusty houses and the silver reflection of the light on the water. His version goes for a big finish that kind of runs against the rest of the song's calm, peaceful vibe, but the song has become a classic of the French chanson tradition and has been used in films and covered countless times.

An English-language version, sung notably by Bobby Darrin in 1959, is called “Beyond the Sea" and focuses not on the beauty of the landscape but on the fact that the narrator's love is overseas; he sings of the happiness they both will feel when he is able to join her. His version was also a hit and has a decidedly swing feel to it.
